How it works

Each month, interest is calculated on the remaining balance using a monthly rate (APR ÷ 12). Your payment covers interest first; the rest reduces principal. Over time, interest goes down and principal goes up.

Why is the interest so high at the start?

The balance is largest at the beginning. As you pay down principal, the interest portion shrinks.

What if I pay extra each month?

Add an extra monthly payment to see payoff faster and total interest drop.

Is this financial advice?

No — this is educational. Verify loan terms, fees, and rounding rules with your lender.
