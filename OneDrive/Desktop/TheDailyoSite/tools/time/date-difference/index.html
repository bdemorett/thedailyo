How it works

This calculator measures the difference between two calendar dates. It shows the gap in total days, weeks and days, and also as a calendar breakdown in years, months, and days.

Total day count
End date − Start date
Inclusive day count
(End date − Start date) + 1
Why can total days differ from months and years?

Because months have different lengths. Calendar difference shows the human-style gap, while total days shows the raw day count.

What does inclusive counting mean?

Inclusive counting includes both the start date and end date as part of the total.

What if I enter the dates backwards?

Auto-order mode swaps them for you. Strict mode preserves the order you entered and may show a negative total day count.
